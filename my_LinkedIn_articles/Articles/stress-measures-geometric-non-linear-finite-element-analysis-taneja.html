1. Introduction

 

In a small deformation analysis, the analysis procedure is carried out only once based on the original undeformed configuration of the structure whereas in a non-linear analysis, the analysis process is repeated at discreet time points taking into account the deformed state of the structure at each discreet time point. The stress measure ‘engineering stress’ that is used in small deformation analysis is based on the original configuration of the structure.

Since, a geometric non-linear analysis involves taking into account the deformed state of the structure at each discreet time point, the engineering stress measure which is used in a small deformation analysis is not applicable in a geometric nonlinear analysis.

Thus, the Cauchy stress which is based on the current configuration of the structure comes into play. However, it is not directly possible to use this stress in the finite element equations as explained in the section 4 of this article. Hence, other stress and strain measures such as the 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress and the Green Lagrangian strain tensor come into existence.

This forms the main aim of this article: to discuss the different stress measures used in geometric nonlinear finite element and the reasons for these different stress measures. Emphasis is laid only on the 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress tensor and the Green Lagrangian strain tensor, though there are other stress and strain measures that are in use in commercial software’s.

I have attempted to logically divide the contents of this article, with:

  • Section 2 explaining the basic problem in a geometric non-linear finite element analysis
  • Section 3 explaining the principle of virtual displacements with a reasonable background
  • Section 4 describes in detail the difficulty in applying the principle of virtual displacements using the Cauchy stress
  • Section 5 defines other stress and strain measures: 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress and the Green Lagrangian strain
  • I have attempted to bring about the physical interpretation of the 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress and the Cauchy stress in section 6.

 

2. Basic problem in a non-linear finite element analysis

 

In my article: https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/solution-strategies-static-non-linear-finite-element-equations-ajay?trk=prof-post , I had tried to emphasize the fact that in a non-linear analysis, the equilibrium of the body considered must be established in the current configuration. That is, we try to equate the external forces and the internal forces corresponding to the deformed state of the structure.

Thus, the aim in a non-linear analysis is to evaluate the equilibrium positions of the complete body at discrete time points 0, Δt, 2Δt, 3Δt… where Δt is an increment of time. To develop the solution strategy, it is assumed that the solution for static variables (i.e. displacements) and kinematic variables (velocities, accelerations) for all the time steps from time 0 to time t, inclusive have been obtained.

Then, the solution process for a typical time step t+ Δt is carried out.

4. Difficulty in the application of the principle of virtual displacements

 

 A fundamental difficulty in the application of the principle of virtual displacement is that the configuration of the body at time t+Δt is not known. This is an important difference compared with the linear analysis in which it is assumed that the displacements are infinitesimally small so that the original configuration is used (when we say original, it means: same area, volume).The continuous change in the configuration of the body entails some important consequences for the development of the incremental analysis procedure.

 For example;

  1. The statement of principle of virtual displacements involves the volume integral at time t+Δt. However, the volume at time t+Δt is not known.
  2. Also, the Cauchy stresses at time t+Δt cannot be obtained by simply adding to the Cauchy stress at time “t” a stress increment that is due to the straining of the material. This is because the Cauchy stress at time “t” and the Cauchy stress at time “t+Δt” are in different configurations as a result of the body undergoing rigid body motion (translation and/or rotation).

6. Physical interpretation of Cauchy stress and the 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress

 

 Following points may be noted with regards to the Cauchy stress and the 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress:

 1. Cauchy stress is measures with reference to the actual configuration of the deformed state of the structure. That is, as the structure traverses from time‘t’ to a time‘t+Δt’, it translates/ rotates (as a rigid body) and deforms. Cauchy stress is measured relative to the actual configuration of the structure at that time step whereas the 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress is measured with reference to the original configuration of the structure at time t=0.

 It should be noted that both the 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress tensor and the Cauchy stress tensor have the same eigen values i.e. the same principal stresses. It is just that the Cauchy tensor is measures with reference to a different configuration (i.e. the actual deformed configuration of the structure at that state) than the original configuration at time t=0.

 2. The 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress may be interpreted as a force in the reference configuration acting on the area in the reference configuration. However, it is not directly interpretable in an experiment.
