1. INTRODUCTION

 1.1 GENERAL

Traditionally, design engineers use rough estimates of effective flexural stiffness EI of beams and columns in the analysis of reinforced concrete structures. The assumed stiffness can affect the structural analysis and design in two significant ways:

 

a) P-δ effects

 

The assumption of lower stiffness for column increases the geometric non linear effects in columns and causes an overall increase in  moments.

b) Internal force distribution

For regular structures it is generally assumed that the internal force distribution is negligibly affected by the stiffness assumption of various structural members. This is more or less the case as long as the relative stiffness of members meeting at a joint essentially remains the same as the structure is subjected to increasing loads. Even in such cases the P-δ effects / geometric non linear effects can cause the enhancement of internal forces at column ends.

Given the above justification, the project examines the variation of flexural stiffness of beam or column section with the parameters like reinforcement ratio, aspect ratio, compression steel ratio, axial load ratio, eccentricity ratio, the compression strength of concrete.The following sections describe the formal definition of the problem.

1.2 CAUSES FOR VARIATION IN FLEXURAL STIFFNESS

In general, it can be said that the variation in flexural stiffness of an RC section (whether beam or column) occurs due to low tensile cracking of concrete. Tensile cracking of concrete on the other hand is likely to be affected by variables like tension steel area, magnitude of axial load, compression steel area, aspect ratio (b/d), equivalent eccentricity ratio (e/D).

The tensile cracking of concrete is likely to be detrimental following yielding of steel which results in a larger drop in the flexural stiffness.

1.4 SCOPE AND OBJECTIVE OF WORK

The purpose of this project is to study the influence of the following parameters on effective stiffness:

  1. The Tension reinforcement ratio, ρ
  2. The Compression steel ratio, ρ′
  3. Aspect ratio, b/d
  4. Compression strength of concreteThe above parameters are considered for beams.

For columns, the parameters selected for study include.

  1. The Gross steel ratio
  2. Axial load ratio P/P
  3. Eccentricity ratio e/D
  4. Compression strength of concrete, f.

An expression for computing the flexural stiffness of beams and column published in the ACI journal  is used and it is found that the results for effective stiffness obtained using the analytical study compares reasonably with those obtained using the expression in the published work.

3.1 GENERAL

 

In order to study the influence of various parameters affecting the flexural stiffness of RC members, the thrust moment curvature relationship for RC sections has been programmed in C language. The program developed gives the moment curvature relationship for a typical RC section for various values of compressive strain in extreme fibre of section upto ultimate strain, for a particular value of axial force. The moment of resistance divided by the corresponding curvature gives the stiffness “EI” at the corresponding strain level for a particular value of axial force.

 

The variation in stiffness by altering the parameters viz. tension steel ratio, compression steel ratio, aspect ratio, axial load ratio, eccentricity ratio, gross steel ratio is then studied.
