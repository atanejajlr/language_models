2. Non-linear finite element analysis: Problem background

 

A non-linear analysis generally involves carrying out the analysis procedure at various time steps from time t=0 to the final time step required in a series of intervals. In each time step (or, a load step), we consider the corresponding equilibrium position of the body and solve the finite element equations and hence the static or/and the kinematic variables.

To arrive at the actual deformed configuration of the body, an iterative procedure is followed and the iterations repeated until the required tolerance concerning the residue (which defines the difference between the external and internal forces) is established.

2.2   Difficulty in the application of the principle of virtual displacements at time ‘t+Δt’

 

An important point to be understood and noted at this stage is; that, it is not possible to apply the equation of the principle of virtual displacements directly at this stage. This is because:

  1. The equation of the principle of virtual displacements involves volume integrals. The integration is performed considering the configuration / volume of the body at time “t+Δt”. However, it should be noted that the volume of the body at time “t+Δt” is not known.
  2. Secondly, it will be observed, as we proceed to formulate the finite element equations, we decompose the stresses and strains at time t+Δt into;
  •               stresses and strains at time ‘t’
  •             stresses and strains at an incremental time step, that  the decomposition gives the net stresses and strains at time ‘t+Δt’

But, it should be understood that the Cauchy stresses at time ‘t+Δt’ cannot be simply obtained by adding to the Cauchy stresses at time ‘t’ a stress increment that is due to the straining of the material. This is because the Cauchy stresses at time t+Δt act on the configuration of the body at time t+Δt which includes the rigid body effects due to a rigid body translation and/or rotation.

3.2  Steps in the Updated Lagrangian formulation

 

As stated before, in the Total Lagrangian formulation the static and kinematic variables are referred to the initial configuration, t=0 whereas in the Updated Lagrangian formulation, the static and the kinematic variables are referred to the last calculated configuration.

 Thus, the two formulations differ in the aspect of the configuration in which the stresses and strains are expressed. IT is possible to get the same values of stress using appropriate transformations from one configuration to another.

 The Updated Lagrangian formulation is based on the same process as the Total Lagrangian formulation except that the stress and strain tensors: 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress and the Green Lagrangian strain tensors will be based on the configuration at time ‘t’ when solving the equation of the principle of virtual displacement at time ‘t+Δt’.

 Thus, the main steps involving the linearization of the equation of the principle of virtual displacements remain same which include;

 

  1. Forming the equation of the principle of virtual displacement.
  2.  Decomposing the stresses and strains into known and unknown increments.
  3. Re-writing the principle of virtual displacements in terms of decomposed strains and stresses.
  4. Linearizing the above equation by ignoring the nonlinear strain terms.

5. Deformation dependent loading (“follower” loading)

 

5.1 Difference between a deformation dependent loading and deformation independent loading

 

In a finite element analysis, several types of loads can be introduced: concentrated loads, body forces, surface loads, etc. In case of surface loads acting normal to the surface during the deformation, these loads are called “pressures”.

Thus, the pressure loads are examples of deformation dependent loading since they would always (i.e. whatever be the deformed state of the structure) act normal to the surface of the body under analysis.

Whereas, the “gravity” loads can be stated as an example of deformation independent loading.
