1.  Introduction

 

This article describes and illustrates and explains in detail the various continuum mechanics variables that are used in the geometric non-linear analysis formulations for finite element analysis. These include;

  1. Deformation gradient tensor,
  2. Right Cauchy Green deformation gradient tensor,
  3. Green Lagrangian strain tensor,
  4. 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress tensor,

 

All the above variables are of utmost importance during the formulation of geometric non-linear finite element equations whether through the total Lagrangian formulation or through the updated Lagrangian formulation.

This article is organized as follows;

 Section 2 of the document describes in detail the definition, math expressions, and illustrative examples concerning the deformation gradient tensor. It is also attempted to point some differences between the deformation gradient tensor and the strain tensor that is used in small deformations.

 Section 3 explains the definition, math expressions and the importance of the Right Cauchy green deformation gradient tensor.

 The Green Lagrangian strain tensor which I had discussed in some detail in my article: https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/stress-measures-geometric-non-linear-finite-element-analysis-taneja?trk=mp-reader-card can be written in several equivalent ways. This forms the discussion and illustrations involving this topic in section 4.

 The 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress that I had discussed in my preceding article (as stated in the above paragraph) is briefly recapitulated in the section 5.

 Finally, some very important properties of the 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress tensor and the Green Lagrangian strain tensor are discussed in section 6.

5.2 Properties of the 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress tensor:

 

Following points may be noted with regards to the 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress tensor;

  1. The stress tensor is symmetric.
  2.  It is invariant under a rigid body motion (translation or rotation)
  3.  The 2nd Piola Kirchhoff stress may be interpreted as a force in the reference configuration acting on the area in the reference configuration. However, it is not directly interpretable in an experiment.
