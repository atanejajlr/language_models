1. INTRODUCTION

This write up attempts to explain the significance of the results of an impulse loading on a single degree of freedom system. Such a load consists of a single principal impulse of arbitrary form and generally is of short duration. Impulsive or shock loads are of great importance in the design criteria of certain classes of structural systems e.g. travelling cranes, vehicles such as trucks and automobiles.

Further, the results of an impulse loading are directly useful in obtaining the response of a system subjected to a force p(t) varying arbitrarily with time. In developing a general solution, p(t) can be interpreted as a sequence of impulses of infinitesimal duration and the response of the system to p(t) is the sum of the responses to individual impulses.

This is known as “convolution” or the “Duhamel” integral- a general result that applies to any linear dynamic system.

Duhamel’s integral provides a general result for evaluating the response of a linear SDOF system to arbitrary force. This result is restricted to linear systems because it is based on the principle of superposition. Thus, it does not apply to structures deforming beyond their linearly elastic limit. IF p(τ) is a simple function, closed form evaluation of the integral is possible. If p(τ) is a complicated function, evaluation of the integral requires numerical methods.
