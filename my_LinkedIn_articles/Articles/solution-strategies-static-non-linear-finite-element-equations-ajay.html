1. INTRODUCTION 

 

In a non-linear finite element analysis procedure, the total load on the structure is divided into a series of load steps and consequently the stiffness characteristics of the structure are updated based on the deformed configuration of the structure in each load step. This requires a re-computation of the stiffness matrix based on the state of the structure during each load step as well as the re-computation of the load vector if the loading on the structure is a deformation dependent loading (often termed as ‘follower forces’ in commercial finite element software’s).

This task to obtain the stiffness matrix based on the current state of the structure is not trivial from the computational point of view and is iterative in nature. The aim of this article is to discuss in detail some of the solution strategies to solve the nonlinear finite element equations; in particular the strategies to obtain the stiffness matrix based on the desired degree of accuracy and thus obtaining the most accurate response of the structure.

There are quite a few solution strategies that are used in commercial finite element software’s, the most common techniques being;

  • Full Newton-Raphson approach,
  • Modified Newton Raphson approach,
  • Initial stress approach.

The aim of this article is to present in detail the physics and the math involved in the above solution approaches for static non-linear finite element analysis.

I have attempted to logically organize the content of this article with;

  • The section 2 and section 3 providing a background of the problem in a general non-linear finite element analysis.
  • It should be noted that the modified Newton-Raphson as well as the initial stress method have their origins from the full Newton-Raphson technique and it is attempted to bring out the difference and the motivation behind each of the techniques in section 4.
  • Section 5 presents in detail the physics and the math behind the Newton-Raphson technique and in section 6 the initial stress and the modified Newton-Raphson technique have been elaborated.

4.1 Motivation of other (slightly different) schemes than the Newton-Raphson’s technique

 

An important point for the solution nonlinear finite element equation;

Is the calculation of t+ΔtF(i-1) from (t+Δt)U(i-1).

Any errors in this calculation will, in general, result in incorrect response prediction.

The correct evaluation of the tangent stiffness matrix (t+Δt)K(i-1) is also crucial. The use of proper tangent stiffness matrix may be necessary for convergence and, in general, will result in less iteration until convergence is reached.

However, because of the expense involved in evaluating and factoring a new tangent stiffness matrix, in practice (as in the Newton Raphson technique), it can be more efficient, depending upon the non-linearity’s present in the analysis, to evaluate the new tangent stiffness matrix only at certain times/load levels.

4.2     Modified Newton Raphson and the initial stress method

 

Specifically, in the modified Newton Raphson method new tangent stiffness matrix iterations is established at the beginning of each load step and in the initial stress method, secant stiffness matrix is used instead of the tangent stiffness matrix.

It should be noted that the question of which scheme to use is a matter of computation efficiency provided that the convergence is reached.

Thus, it may be stated that, whichever iterative technique is used, the basic requirements in the solution of non-linear finite element equilibrium equations are:

  1. Evaluation of the (tangent) stiffness matrix corresponding to a given state
  2. The evaluation the nodal force vector corresponding to the stresses in that state (the state being tU or t+ΔtU(i-1), i=1,2,3..). That means, we are using the displacement vector of the preceding iteration whenever iteration number i>1 and when i=1, we use the displacement vector of the preceding time step/load level.

It may be noted that both the modified Newton Raphson and the initial stress method are just a convenient extension of the Newton Raphson iteration scheme- they differ from the Newton Raphson iteration scheme through the consideration of when the stiffness matrix is updated: for the Newton Raphson scheme the stiffness matrix being updated in every iteration, in the modified Newton Raphson, the stiffness matrix being updated only at the beginning of each load step (or time) and in the initial stress the stiffness corresponds to the initial stiffness at time t=0 i.e. the stiffness matrix corresponding to the undeformed configuration.

Considering the inherent importance of the Newton Raphson iteration scheme, the topic is dealt in considerable detail in the subsequent sections.

5. MATHEMATICAL AND PHYSICAL BACKGROUND BEHIND THE NEWTON RAPHSON’S ITERATION TECHNIQUE

 

5.1   The Newton Raphson approach

 

In numerical analysis, the Newton-Raphson method is a method for finding successively better approximations to the roots (or zeroes) of a real valued function;

That is, we need to find the value of ‘x’ for which f(x) =0.

The idea of the method is as follows;

One starts with an initial guess which is reasonably close to the true root, then the function is approximated by its tangent line (which can be computed using the tools of calculus) and one computes the x-intercept of the tangent line (which is easily done using elementary algebra). This x intercept will be a better approximation of the function’s root than the original guess and the method can be iterated.

5.5    Convergence in Newton Raphson iteration technique

 

Following points may be noted with regards to the convergence in Newton Raphson iterations;

  1. The approximations obtained using Newton Raphson’s iterations exhibit quadratic convergence if the approximations are “close” to the root. The convergence rate is quire rapid one the quadratic convergence is obtained.
  2. However, if the first approximation us “far” from the root, Newton Raphson iteration may not converge to the desired value.

6.     INITIAL STRESS METHOD AND MODIFIED NEWTON RAPHSON METHOD

 

Considering the Newton-Raphson iteration process, it is recognized that in general, the major computational cost per iteration lies in the calculation of the tangent stiffness matrix. Since the calculation can be quite expensive, the use of a modification of the full Newton Raphson algorithm can be effective.

However, the initial stress method may require several more iterations than a full Newton-Raphson method and thus converge very slowly or even diverge.

 

6.1   Modified Newton Raphson

 

The modified Newton Raphson is an approach somewhat between the full Newton-Raphson iteration technique and the initial stress.

In the modified Newton Raphson, the stiffness is matrix is updated at the beginning of each load step only and not during the subsequent iterations that follow in the load step.

6.2   Full Newton-Raphson vs. Modified Newton Raphson and Initial stress method

 

It may be noted that the initial stress method and the modified Newton-Raphson method much less costly than the full Newton-Raphson method per iteration. However, many more iterations are necessary to achieve the same accuracy and more importantly the initial stress method and the Modified Newton Raphson “cannot” exhibit quadratic convergence when closer to the root.
