2.   What are Randomly Varying Stresses?

 Suppose that a part (at a critical location) instead of being subjected to a fully reversible stress history block composed of “n” cycles or a block of similar stress pattern through the whole mission cycle (as in case of fluctuating stresses) is subjected to a time line of stresses exhibiting many and different peaks and valleys as shown below;

Figure 2: Fluctuating varying stresses

In this case, the stress pattern through the cycle is not uniform (as in case of Fluctuating Stresses – see figure 1). Such irregular stress pattern may be termed as: ‘fluctuating varying stress’ pattern or a randomly varying stress pattern.

5.   Limitation of Miners Rule

 The limitation of Miner’s rule can be described as below;

a)    Firstly, the Miner’s rule does not take into account the order in which the cycles were applied.

b)    That is: in some cases the cycle causing damage to the part might result in reduction of the endurance limit of the material. For example, in the case above (section 4), the last cycle does not cause any damage as the stress points (mid-range, alternating stress) lie in the infinite life region. However, it may so happen, that as a result of the damage caused due to the preceding cycles there might be a reduction in the endurance limit of the materials in effect resulting in a calculated damage in the stress pattern D in the above example. Miner’s Rule does not account for this.

Calculating the number of cycles / cycle counting given a randomly varying stress pattern and then arriving at the maximum and minimum stresses is a complex process.
