1.  INTRODUCTION

This write-up concerns with the Response history analysis procedure to calculate the structural response as a function of time when the system is subjected to a given ground acceleration u_g(t). 

The discussion in this article involves the response of MDOF system to a ground acceleration caused due to an earthquake. The concept of Response history analysis which is in fact is the modal analysis procedure carried out when the structure is subjected to a ground acceleration during an earthquake is discussed in some detail.

It will be explained during the course of this article that in case of structures subjected to ground acceleration u_g(t)  , the equation of motion is the same as if the structure was subjected to external forces = -mj* u_g(t),  where ‘j’ corresponds to a DOF of the structure and . Therefore, determining the response of a system subjected to an earthquake excitation by the response history analysis is the same as carrying out a modal analysis of the structure, when the structure is subjected to effective earthquake forces   -mj* u_g(t) and u_g(t) represents the ground acceleration.

This article is organized as follows;                                                       

Section 2 of this article revisits the equation of motion of a SDOF system subjected to a ground acceleration. Section 3 concerns the equation of motion of a MDOF system subjected to ground acceleration, the concepts described in section 2 are extended further to MDOF systems, fundamentally, it may noticed that the scalar quantities represented in section 2 are replaced by matrices.

Section 4 involves the response history analysis which essentially is the modal analysis procedure to determine the response of a structure to earthquake induced ground motion  identical at all support points of the structure.

It may be appreciated that the explanation concerning the determination of response of a system subjected to ground acceleration cannot be possible without the math involving important linear algebra concepts. This will be evident from the discussions in section 4. Therefore, section 6 which lists ALL the interpretations based on the math presented is section 4 has been included.

Finally section 7 summarized the various steps involved in the response / time history analysis.

6.  INTERPRETATIONS OF THE ABOVE MATH

 Following interpretations may be drawn from the math presented in the preceding sections;

1. The equations of motion of a MDOF system are generally coupling.

2. Coupling is manifest in form of the structural matrices being non-diagonal.

3. Mode superposition is a method which transforms the equations of motion from the physical coordinate system into another coordinate system. In this new coordinate system, the equations of motion are uncoupled.

4. This new coordinate system is called the ‘modal’ or ‘normal’ coordinate system.

5. The transformation matrix is the “modal” and each column of the modal matrix represents a vector called as the “modal” vector.

6. Mode shapes are solutions of the free vibration response of the structure wherein all the DOF’s of the structure oscillate simple harmonically.

7. In the transformed coordinate system each equation is solved as a SDOF system

8. Once the solution is obtained, the total response in the physical coordinate system is obtained by considering the contribution from each mode.

7.  SUMMARY OF STEPS

The response of a structure subjected to earthquake ground motion can be computed as a function of time by the procedure detailed in the preceding steps which is summarized in a step by step form as below;

1. Define the ground acceleration numerically at every time step

2. Define the structural properties;

  a. Determine the mass matrix m and the stiffness matrix k

  b.Estimate the modal damping ratios.

3. Determine the natural frequencies (natural periods ) and the natural modes of vibration

4. Determine the modal components of the effective earthquake force distribution.

5. Compute the response contribution for the nth mode using the following steps which are repeated for all the modes, n = 1 ,2,….., N

a. Perform static analysis of the building subjected to lateral forces to determine the modal static response for each desired response quantity r

b. Determine the pseudo acceleration response of the nth mode SDOF system to using the numerical time stepping procedures

c. Determine rn (t)

6. Combine the modal contributions to determine the total response.

Since, only a few modes contribute to the response, steps 3, 4 and 5 may be done for the first few modes. This has not been discussed in this article
