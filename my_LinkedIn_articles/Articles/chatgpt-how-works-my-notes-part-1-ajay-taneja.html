What is ChatGPT?

Language Models â€“ What are Language Models?

Language models are models which have an inherent understanding of language in a mathematical sense. Language models understand the probability distribution of a sequence of words. That is: given a context of words that have preceded it, language models will know what word (or, precisely the token) to generate next. One can get language models to handle specific tasks like:

  • Question and Answering
  • Text Summarization
  • Machine Translation
  • More.

Some of the language models include the following:

1.   Bidirectional Encoding Representation of Transformers [BERT]

2.   Generative Pretrained Transformers [GPT]

3.   Recurrent Neural Networks [RNN]

4.   Long Short-Term Memory [LSTM] networks

The Language models are pre-trained on general language data and then fine-tuned depending upon the task we want to solve. 2.   How is ChatGPT trained?

Let us go a bit deeper into ChatGPT. ChatGPT has been trained using Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF). The training of ChatGPT can be summarized into 3 basic steps:

Step 1:

An initial model of ChatGPT was trained using supervised fine tuning (SFT) â€“ in which there was already a pre-trained language model â€“ the pre-trained language model in case of ChatGPT was Generative Pre-Trained Transformer (GPT).

Fine tuning of GPT has been carried out using human AI trainers that provided the questions and answers. That is: the AI trainers â€“ the labellers write a prompt and also write the response to the answer of the prompts â€“ this is the supervised fine tuning of the GPT model.

Step 2:

In the next step of ChatGPT training, a comparison data was collected which consisted of 2 or more â€œmodelâ€ responses ranked by quality. In this case, the responses were that of the fine tuned GPT model but were ranked by human AI trainers.

This prompt / response / ranking is used to train another GPT model which is called the â€œRewards Modelâ€. The input to the â€œRewards Modelâ€ is the prompt and one of the responses by the supervised fine tined model of Step 1 and the output is going to be a reward which is the quantification of how good the response was. 

Step 3:

In the step 3 of the GPT training, an unseen prompt is taken and passed through a copy of the supervised fine-tuned model and then through the rewards model of step 2 to get a rank.

Whatever rank is obtained is used to further fie tune the model to create a better response. This process helps the GPT model to create more factual responses, more coherent responses with hat of the question and eliminating any biased or adverse responses.
